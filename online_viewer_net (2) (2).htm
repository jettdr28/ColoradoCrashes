20 Most Dangerous Intersections in the Denver Metro Area

Displays the 20 intersections with the most crashes (2022–2024) within 20 miles of Denver across Adams, Arapahoe, Broomfield, Denver, Douglas, and Jefferson counties.

  • Hover over a bubble to see the total number of crashes, crashes by year, and the intersection’s rank.
  • If intersections have the same number of crashes over the past three years, ties in rank are broken based on the number of crashes in 2024.
  • Use your mouse to zoom in and out to examine key intersections in more detail.
  • If you zoom in closely on a street or intersection, it will highlight in blue. You can click on it to view what the area actually looks like. You can also pan around to explore the surrounding area.

Note: Tooltip may take an extra second to load the first time due to initial data caching. Subsequent hovers will be much faster.

Colorado Crash Totals

All navigation arrows are located in the top-right corner of the chart.

Displays total reported crashes across Colorado by year. It offers interactive drill-down capabilities to explore crash data at the county and city levels.

  • Click the double arrows to explore crash totals by county.
  • Click the single up arrow to return to the previous level.
  • On the original view (by year), clicking the single down arrow, then selecting a specific bar, will show county-level crash totals for that year.
  • Hover over any bar to see a breakdown of crashes by city within that county, usually the top 10 cities, or all cities if fewer than 10.
